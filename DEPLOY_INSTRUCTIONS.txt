Awesome! If your dashboard loaded, that means your React app successfully pulled data from your serverless AWS backend!

The final step of Stage 2 is to deploy your frontend so it's hosted on the internet instead of your local machine.

### Step 5: Deploy Frontend to S3

We will use Amazon S3 to host your React static files.

1. Build your production React app. Open a new terminal tab, make sure you are in the `frontend/` directory, and run:
   `npm run build`
   (This creates a `dist` folder with your optimized frontend files).

2. Go to the AWS Management Console in your web browser (https://console.aws.amazon.com/s3).
3. Click "Create bucket".
4. Bucket name: Name it something unique like `finstream-dashboard-YOURNAME` (bucket names must be globally unique!).
5. In the "Object Ownership" section, select "ACLs enabled".
6. Uncheck the "Block all public access" box and acknowledge the warning.
7. Click "Create bucket" at the bottom.

8. Click on your newly created bucket to open it.
9. Go to the "Properties" tab, scroll to the very bottom, and click "Edit" on "Static website hosting".
10. Select "Enable". Type `index.html` for both the Index document and Error document. Click "Save changes".

11. Now, upload your files! Go to the "Objects" tab inside your bucket.
12. Click "Upload", and drag everything INSIDE your `frontend/dist` folder into the bucket.
13. Scroll down, expand "Permissions", and under "Predefined ACLs", select "Grant public-read access".
14. Click "Upload".

Once it's done, go back to the "Properties" tab, scroll to the bottom, and click the "Bucket website endpoint" link. Your FinStream dashboard is now live on the internet!
